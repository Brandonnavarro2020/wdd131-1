Welcome to Madrid

A commuter town near Bogotá with a strong flower production industry and growing urban life.

About Madrid

Location and elevation: Madrid is located in the Cundinamarca department on the Bogotá savanna at approximately 2,554 meters (8,379 ft) above sea level and within the Western Savanna Province.

Population and growth: Recent estimates place the municipality population around 140,000 residents; it is one of the fast-growing and most populous municipalities near Bogotá within the metropolitan area.

History: The settlement dates to the colonial period (founded in the 16th century) and later became an organized municipality; its modern development is tied to regional transport links and suburban expansion.

Economy: Madrid is well known for flower production, especially roses exported internationally; the municipality also hosts manufacturing, logistics and retail facilities that serve the Bogotá region.
